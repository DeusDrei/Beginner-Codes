Wakana is quite tall for his age, standing at at least 180 centimeters.
(When Marin asked him his height, he was interrupted before he could finish saying, meaning he's likely taller than 180cm.)
He has short, black hair and grey eyes, along with a tiny mole on the outer lower corner of his right eye.
Outside of his school uniform, he is always wearing a samue,
a general work outfit consisting of a blue suit jacket and pants, with a simple white shirt underneath.
He wears wooden sandals and also tends to wear a white headband while working.
When not wearing his headband, he tends to have it over his shoulders.


Wakana's main goal in life is to become a hina doll craftsman (a kashira-shi - 頭師) like his grandfather.
He is very knowledgeable and loving towards the dolls his grandfather’s store makes and sells.
He also has his own and cherishes it greatly, to the point where he keeps it on his desk by his bedside and even talks to it.



HINA-DOLLS are a Girl's Festival (March 3) decoration.
In Japan, they are called "HINA-NINGYOU" or "OHINA-SAMA".
For the first time, these displays are decorated on the first girl's day after a baby girl was born for her.
Then, the displays are decorated for a month every year. Some customers sometimes asked me,
how long does it decorate, how old she would be.
I would answer that she can decide it by herself because the displays are her own things.
Hina dolls are displayed to pray for her good health and good fortunes.
